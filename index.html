The CIT Elevator

An exploration of an unnecessarily complex interface

ft. two very unlucky cit goers

the_interface

the inner workings of a surprisingly frustrating interface

While the average user will have no trouble getting to their destination, there exist some extreme edge cases when using this elevator. The CIT elevator has a pretty simple interface, no different than your average elevator. However, the one major quirk is the addition of swiping. During the day, up until around 6pm, all floors are accessible without swiping. After 6 or so, floors 3-5 require a swipe which only professors, grad students, and ta's have. Despite coming daily to this god forsaken building, I have no idea at exactly what time the access shuts off, but for the sake of this project, we can just say 6pm.

Primary issue: only certain people have access, but the accessibility rules are unclear

three_observations

people were not very receptive to the idea of me loitering in the elevator

user_1
- Enters elevator on the 2nd floor going up
- Swipes, fails, and presses 4th floor button
- Button fades and user goes back to panel
- Elevator stars going back down
- Swipes but gets denied
- Swipes again but with more force
- Swipes again but much slower
- Card reader lights up green and user presses button
- Elevator rocks as it transitions from going down to up, user jolts
Has done this process many times as they are a grad student who frequents the upper floors, but just fumbled with the card reader on this occasion.
user_2
- Gets in on 1st floor and pressed 3rd floor button
- Button flickers, and elevator does not move
- Tries to swipe and gets denied
- Tries two more times, then annoyedly asks for help
- Inspects the small picture of the card to figure out the orientation
- Flips card, swipes, and fails
- Swipes two more times but faster
- Sighs and tries a slower swipe
Knows that the swipe access is required, thought they had it, but was confused when it didn't seem to work for them.
user_3
- User presses the button for the 4th floor
- Button flickers and elevator starts moving
- Elevator stops on the 2nd floor
- Someone gets in and presses 1st floor
- They get out and original user looks around puzzled
- User presses 4th floor button
- It flickers and nothing happens, then asks me why
- They try swiping a couple of times
- I end up swiping for them
Was told to go to the 4th floor for a meeting. Had no idea that they needed swipe access. Didn't understand why it's even gated behind swipe access, and was generally confused by the experience.
